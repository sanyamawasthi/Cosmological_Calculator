Cosmological Redshift :

In normal on-going life, we have redshift or blueshift arising due to relative motion between objects. Similarly, in cosmology, we have cosmological redshift which not just an indication of relative radial speed between galaxies but can also give an idea of how much one has gone into the past. More the value of Cosmological redshift, more old universe we are looking at. This is an important parameter to know the age, relative speed, and distance from observer to the target galaxy.

Density

Radiation Density

Dark Energy

As universe expands, this expansion accelerates too. Now supernovae were being studied and their brightness was calculated. In reality, they were fainter than the brightness recorded. It concluded to presence of new density called dark energy density.

Age of universe and Redshift

To be put simply, more the redshift observed from the galaxy, more old galaxy or universe we are looking at and near it is to big bang. For example, galaxy with redshift=7.15 tells that we are looking at galaxy when universe was 740 million years old.

CMB Temperature

CMB (Cosmic Microwave Background) is the radiation that has filled the universe and has been present from the starting of the universe. Now as universe expanded, the temperature of the CMB also has decreased. Using redshift, one can determine the temperature for the universe at that redshift. For present universe, the temperature is 2.3 K.
